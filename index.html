About Me

Hi there! A common misconception is that fashion designers simply “draw pretty things.” On the contrary, fashion designers are the common denominator connecting technical feasibility, business goals, and customer needs. My designs must intuitively predict what users want, provide technical specifications to factories, and be prototyped to perfection. For 13 years as an apparel designer, I have used qualitative and quantitative research, to critically think about all factors and variables when analyzing data. “How can I replicate a best-selling design for the next season? Why didn’t the consumer react to a silhouette? Were technical fit issues missed in production? Did weather factor into fabrications and delivery dates? What influences my customer? Where is the magical price point?” I also must confess, I do draw pretty things!
